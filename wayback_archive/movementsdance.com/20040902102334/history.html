History

Movements: the Afro-Caribbean Dance Ensemble is an internationally acclaimed dance company based in Edmonton, Alberta, that is dedicated to educating the public and building an appreciation and understanding of dance, African and Caribbean culture and multiculturalism by:

  • Offering dance classes and workshops for adults, youths and children
  • Performing at community and national events
  • Collaborating with other dance companies
  • Participating in major Alberta and Canadian festivals
  • Making presentations on dance and culture with schools and diverse organizations
  • Contributing to the community through charity work and fundraising

Movements was formed on February 11th, 1990, by Sharlene Thomas, who remains its Artistic Director. The company was founded on the day former South African President Nelson Mandela was freed from prison, and is built on the same foundation of strength and hope shared by this visionary world leader. Sharlene's personal vision of unity, through diversity, is an integral part of the work of Movements. A renowned producer, speaker and community leader, Sharlene was named Global TV Woman of Vision in 1998.

A board-governed, and volunteer organization, Movements leads by example. It has made a positive impact on Edmonton's youth and the community at large, as evidenced by this quote from Thomas' mentor, Nelson Mandela, in his missive.

“…yours is a truly noble cause.”
Nelson Mandela, Former President of South Africa
