G.E.T. FEST ~ empowering and motivating Edmonton's youth
An extraordinary event and journey of self-discovery that won’t soon be forgotten. Youth, ages 12-22, are brought together in exploration of their roots, their interests, their future and the world around them. Throughout the two weeks, they will meet, enjoy, and develop relationships with others.

The festival consists of four basic events:

  • Two-week long motivational presentations
  • Edmonton's Rising Star - Talent Competition
  • Day-long forum with presentations and workshops
  • Saturday Evening "Jam"

The educational and entertaining activities will benefit their physical, mental, social and emotional well-being. The festival will heighten the awareness of our rich Black History and showcase the future leaders of tomorrow.

History:
Founded in 1926, Negro History Week is celebrated annually around the world. In 1976, it became known as the International Black History Month Festival. Movements was founded on February 11, 1990, the day former President Nelson Mandela was released from prison. Movements makes indelible contributions to the City of Edmonton with its dance theatre productions that celebrate this auspicious Festival on a yearly basis. Some of our highlights include Remembering Amber Valley, From Then to Now – Take Me Back Africa, and A Tribute to Mandela.

G.E.T FEST is organized and supported by cultural, community, government and corporate organizations. Movements partnered with Edmonton schools and education community to bring a series of speakers and workshops. In keeping with our mandate to educate the young and embrace Canada’s rich multicultural diversity, Movements feels aptly suited to produce the first Annual Youth festival. This inspiring initiative gives Movements the opportunity to form new and lasting partnerships within the community, as we Get Edmonton Together - one youth at a time.

Advance auditions for the Edmonton's Rising Star Talent Competition are on Sunday January 18th at Rum Jungle in West Edmonton Mall (2687, 8882-170 St.) from 11:00 a.m. until 5:00 p.m.. Finalists will go on to compete in the Edmonton's Rising Star Talent Competition. For more information or to book an appointment please call Jesse at 460-8185 or get_fest@hotmail.com.

Get Involved:
Individual School Tours:
Monday, January 26-Thursday, January 29 (Junior high schools)
Monday, February 2-Friday, February 6 (High schools)
Motivational speakers and celebrity guests will visit junior and senior high schools throughout Edmonton. Youth will learn about Black History Month and issues important to them, including performing your personal best, and the activities planned for the youth festival. The panel includes motivational speaker Sharlene Thomas, Global TV Woman of Vision; an outstanding individual, Jesse Lipscombe, and a number of community leaders.

Get Started:
Youth Forum: Friday, January 30
1,000 students, aged 11-14, will be transported from across the city to the Shaw Conference Centre for two, three-hour motivational forums. These action-packed seminars will include an exciting audio/visual presentation, a distinguished panel of celebrity and guest speakers including former Alberta Human Rights Commissioner and President of Vision TV Fil Fraser, motivational speaker Sharlene Thomas, Edmonton Eskimo Henry Williams, Georges Laraque of the Edmonton Oilers, and Canadian Idol judge Farley Flex. There will also be a performance component comprising of Movements: the Afro-Caribbean Dance Ensemble, a singing group, and the N.A.A.R.R organization performing a skit.

Get On Stage:
Edmonton's Rising Star Talent Competition: Friday, January 30
On stage at Victoria School for the Performing Arts Theatre (10210-108 Ave.), this high profile event showcases Edmonton's youth musicians, singers, and dancers. Featuring the Canadian Idol judge Farley Flex, a panel of celebrity judges including Shannon Tyler from 96X, Edmonton Oiler Georges Laraque, and the popular DJ Dahved Levy from New York City's WBLS 107.5 FM, will proudly present the first prize winner a round trip ticket & one night hotel accommodation, production of a 3-song demo, and tickets to the Canadian Idol finals…… all in Toronto. Make your vote count in naming Edmonton’s newest RISING STAR. Don’t miss your chance. Runner-up prizes will also be awarded. Open to youth ages 15-22.

Get Up: “Celebrating me”
Youth Symposium: Saturday, January 31 - 9:00 a.m. - 1:45 p.m.
Engaging & exciting! The first 200 kids to arrive before 8.45 am receive door prizes including gift certificates for free food, CDs, and clothing stores.
The Symposium kicks-off at 9:00 a.m. sharp, at the Delta Centre Suite Hotel, with a panel of not to be missed celebrity speakers. Following the panel are presentations and participatory workshops involving the concerns and issues of the youth. It is open to all youth aged 12-22.

The workshops will highlight:

  1. Drugs & Alcohol - S.A.D.D.
  2. Gangs & Violence - Edmonton Police
  3. Sex & Dating - RespectEd, Canadian Red Cross
  4. STD's & Birth Control - Planned Parenthood
  5. Racism & Human Rights
  6. Who Wants To Be A Millionaire - Your Financial Future
  7. YOUTH TALK BACK - Where the youth get a chance to voice their opinions with

Wrapping up at 1:45 p.m., all participants will leave to attend the International Black History Month Festival opening ceremonies at City Hall.

Get Together:
Saturday Evening Jam: January 31st - 5:30 p.m. - 11:00 p.m.
Also held at the Delta Centre Suite Hotel in downtown Edmonton, this evening event begins with a comedy show and culminates in an evening Jam. The entertainers include the “Edmonton's Rising Star” winner, JAENESS, the MacDonald’s “I’m Lovin’ it Dancers” and Supreme Team, and other performers. Holdin' it down is Urban Metropolis Sound Crew. This event is open to youth ages12-22.
