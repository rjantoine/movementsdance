on

at

(10233 Jasper Avenue)
 

We cordially invite you to join us at our 'East Meets West' Fall Fundraiser, as we anticipate our 15th year anniversary in 2005. This show will be an explosive cultural fusion of East Indian/West Indian and East African/West African dance and music. It will be fluid, exhilarating, and bursting with colour, rhythm and sound.

Journey with us from the Ganges to the Nile . The performers will be the award-winning Movements: The Afro-Caribbean Dance Ensemble and the Usha Kala Niketan East Indian Dancers, with narration by Charlie West from FM 105.9. An evening not to be missed!

G.E.T. Fest

 

MOVEMENTS: THE AFRO-CARIBBEAN DANCE ENSEMBLE
Presents
G.E.T. FEST
“Getting Edmonton Together Youth Festival”
February 14 – February 19, 2005

A Multicultural/Educational Experience

In celebration of The International Black History Month Festival

G.E.T. FEST ~ empowering and motivating Edmonton's youth
An extraordinary event and journey of self-discovery that won’t soon be forgotten. Youth, ages 12-22, are brought together in exploration of their roots, their interests, their future and the world around them. Throughout the week, they will meet, enjoy, and develop relationships with others.

The educational and entertaining activities will benefit their physical mental, social and emotional well-being. The festival will heighten the awareness of our rich Black History and showcase the future leaders of tomorrow.

History:
Founded in 1926, Negro History Week is celebrated annually around the world. In 1976, it became known as the International Black History Month Festival. Movements was founded on February 11, 1990, the day former President Nelson Mandela was released from prison.

The ensemble has made an indelible contribution to the City of Edmonton with its dance theatre productions which celebrate this auspicious Festival on a yearly basis. Past highlights include Remembering Amber Valley, From Then to Now – Take Me Back Africa, and A Tribute to Mandela.

G.E.T FEST is organized and supported by cultural, community, government and corporate organizations. Movements partnered with Edmonton schools and education community to bring a series of speakers and workshops. In keeping with our mandate to educate the young and embrace Canada’s rich multicultural diversity, Movements feels aptly suited to produce the first Annual Youth festival. This inspiring initiative gives Movements the opportunity to form new and lasting partnerships within the community, as we Get Edmonton Together - one youth at a time.
